(1)

“So you’re finally awake.”

When I open my eyes, I find myself lying on a tiny, dirty bed, with a bunch of strange people surrounding me. The air smells like rotten eggs, making me feel kind of sick. And the man speaking to me, looks like in his fortieth, wears a pair of filthy glasses; he has only one arm left, with the long sleeve of his coad dangling on the other side.

“Where am I--?” I try to sit up but my whole body is so stiff and numb. I think I must have been lying for a long time. The old man takes off his glasses, and stares at me like he just heard the most stupid question in the world.

“Don’t you remember what happened, Link?”

It gives me a shock when I hear the name, which sounds so familiar, and I know he’s calling me. My fragmented memory starts to reassemble-

(2)

My name is Link. When I was ten years old, I was caught in a traffic accident- that part of memory has been blurry, and all I remember is that I wake up in the hospital seeing the doctors, my friends and family screaming, crying and trying to hug me. Everyone says it’s a miracle, that I was caught into such a big accident and still managed to survive somehow, without even getting any disability.

The recovery went very smoothly, as I was able to leave the hospital and go back to school within several months; when I thought everything was over and ready to return to my ordinary life, something happened that changed my entire life.

(3)

My classmates still remembered me- actually, after such a long time we didn’t see each other, and with the fact that I just recovered from a huge accident, everyone was even showing extra kindness to me, which made me become the center of attention everywhere I went. I even started to think it’s possibly a good thing to be caught in that accident.

During lunch,  I was surrounded by my friends who were eager to know how I could survive it and came back to school so soon; everytime I had to explain that it was all about fortune, and enjoyed how they were amazed and jealous of me for having such an extraordinary experience. I was so immersed in such joy that I didn’t even hear the bell rang- when I realized it, I was already late for my psychology class. I grabbed my bag, rushed out of the cafeteria, failed to notice there was someone behind the cafeteria door.

(4)

I directly rushed into him- my geology teacher, Mr.A; he was holding a water bottle and all of his class materials. Normally he would definitely go mad at me for hitting him, but something magical happened- at the moment I hit him, I strange feeling came to my mind, and suddenly the world went slow- he dropped his water bottle, but in the eyes of mine, the bottle was like doing a slow motion, still in the air; I tried to reach the bottle before it hit the ground, and I found I was able to move my arm with a normal speed, allowing me to captured the bottle even before it hit the ground; I can even see how Mr.A’s facial expression changed so slowly, from anger to astonishment.

(5)

That was the first time when I awakened my super power. But starting from then, I became more prudent and cautious- I tried to hide my special ability, because I didn’t want to be seen as a weirdo or a monster. However, this ability of mine really brought me much convenience, as I became adept at using it: when I tried to focus and excite myself, the time seemed like frozen to me, so that I can utilize it to do many extra things- I got almost infinite time for a school quiz or test, so it was such a piece of cake for me to earn a high grade at school. My life became much more smoother- everyone around me call me a genius, for I can remember any hard concepts within a few seconds; my physical ability was also increased into a non-human level, that I think I can easily won any golden medal in any sport competition, or break any world records, as long as I try- but I chose not too. I still kept living my life in an ordinary way. Sometimes I used my ability to help people, without getting noticed of it. I started to get used to such a life.

(6)

One day when the school was over, I decided to take the subway home because there happened to be something wrong with my car, that it didn’t move when I stepped on the gas pedal. There was no one else in the train, which was pretty rare at that time period.

The train started moving. As it went faster and faster, I had a feeling that something was wrong; there was a weird smell, like gasoline, and a strange noise was getting louder and louder. Suddenly I heard an explosion, and the whole carriage got enclosed by fire.

(7）

“ Looks like you get back some memories, huh?” The man smiles at me. “It has been ten years since that explosion- in other words, you’ve been sleeping for ten years.”

“Who are you? What is going -”

“The frontal lobe,” ignoring my questions, the man continues his speech, “It is the part of our brain that is responsible for voluntary movement, recognition and higher-level thinking. It controls your muscle movement, and also your logic and reasonings.”

“ I suppose that you remembered that car accident when you were a kid. During the surgery, you received a special treatment - they used a special radiation that could alter the function of your frontal lobe.”

“Radiation?”

“The SH-Radiation. It has been developing for a long time, since the very time we human beings had discovered biological-psychology. The SH-Radiation is able to boost people’s reacting speed, reasoning ability and physical ability by altering some biological functions of their frontal lobe and nerve system, therefore arousing the full potential of human brains. This is the reason why you can think, move and analyze at an incredibly high rate.”

“So that-”

“Right. Link, you are the first test subject who received the SH-Radiation Treatment.” He says.

I try to stay calm. “Why should I believe in you? Who are you anyway? I’m going home-”

“I’m afraid you cannot.” He sighs. “The world has changed dramatically when you were sleeping.”

(8)

“Your case showed that the SH-Radiation Treatment was a success, and quickly this treatment was put into usage. People who received this treatment had gained incredible abilities far beyond our imagination, exceeding human limits.”

“Isn’t that a good thing?”

“However, the treatment was also very expensive; only the rich people can afford such a price. Starting from billionaires, and then entrepreneurs, merchants, celebrities, finally lawyers and doctors- people who received the SH-Radiation became literally supermen- their intellectual and physical abilities surpassed the ordinary people by much, so they could have overwhelming advantages when competing with ordinary people. They were referred to as the ‘New Generation’.”

“-‘New Generation-?’”

“That’s right. With their overwhelming abilities, the New Generation was able to take control over more than 95.7% of the land, wealth and properties on earth- and the number is still increasing- in contrast, the normal people have to give up their living spaces and properties, only work at the bottom of the society, doing heavy labors and struggling simply to survive.”

“Isn’t that unlawful--?”

“Nothing they did is against the law- actually, the New Generation has total control over politics; the laws are made to protect their interests to start with.”

“In that case, why don’t people just revolt?”

“There were rebellions- however, the New Generation also controls the army. Plus, with their superhuman intelligence and physical abilities, we have no chance to win.”

“So you are-”

“I was one of the psychologists who developed the SH-Radiation Project. And this place is one of the few places that are still not taken over by the New Generation.” He looks around, “The people here were the remaining people who did not become ‘supermen’.”

But I don’t see many people around. “Where are the others? There should be lots more poor people who cannot afford that SH-Radiation thing.”

“Most of them are dead. They either cannot afford the food to maintain their life or cannot withstand the heavy labor and chose to commit suicide.”

He turns to me. “But we still have hope.”

(9)

“You mean-”

“Link, you are our final hope. Even though the New Generation do have strong power, it’s still no match to yours.” He says.

“How do you know that?”

“As I said, I participated in the development of the SH-Radiation, so that I know. When the SH-Radiation was put into usage, its intensity was deducted by a large amount in order to avoid potential damage to the brain. In other words, your ability is much stronger than any one from the New Generation. These people saw you as a threat to them, so they had plotted the explosion to put you into death. However, you miraclely survived, and I brought you here without their awareness.”

“Now it’s your time to choose: would you go and join the New Generation, or help us to change this country?”
